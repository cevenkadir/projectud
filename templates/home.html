What is projectμd?

It is a project whose scope is directional muon detection, i.e., detecting a type of subatomic particle which is created in the upper parts of the atmosphere along with the information of its zenith angle.


This project is a part of PHYS 212 class in Bilkent University, Turkey. Normally, testing the theory of relativity is somewhat difficult for an undergraduate project. However, projectud offers an easy and low-cost method for that.

Theory

Thanks to the special relativity, lifetimes of muons are much longer due to their speeds close to speed of light. Majority of the muons that reach to earth are created about an altitude of 15km from ground, due to the interaction between cosmic ray and particles in atmosphere. To test this phenomenon, arriving muons should be detected and their angles should be determined.


CMOS camera sensor is used to detect cosmic rays. Sensor is isolated to light and cosmic rays other muons, with lead plates and black tape. When an excitation is detected in the sensor, picture is saved. Direction of the muons are determined from the captured images. Longer trace means more horizontal angle due to Pythagorean theorem.
