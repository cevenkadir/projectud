What is ?

It is a project that its scope is directional muon detection, i.e., detecting a type of sub-atomic particle created in the upper parts of the atmosphere along —muons— with the information of its zenith angle by using a PlayStation Eye Camera.

WHAT

remove

Motivation

This project was built as a part of the modern physics course —PHYS 212— in Bilkent University, Turkey. Normally, testing the theory of relativity is somewhat difficult for an undergraduate project; however, offers an easy and low-cost method for that.

Why muon detection matters?

Muons are among the most astonishing particles, historically, since they have been helping scientists to test and understand Einstein’s theory of special relativity. Therefore, muon detection is a useful method for the science of relativity considering that the newest technologies of our age can make the experimentation in this area much easier.

Theory

The majority of muons are produced at about an altitude of 15 km from ground as the decay products of cosmic rays interacting with the particles in the atmosphere of the Earth. Owing to the special relativity, the lifetimes of these muons moving near the speed of light are much longer. In order to test this phenomenon, the incoming muons ought to be detected so that their zenith angle can be determined.

Collecting data

The CMOS image sensor of the camera is used to detect cosmic rays. It is also isolated from visible light and cosmic rays rather than muons, with lead plates and a bunch of cellotapes. When a sparkle is detected, the following picture is saved if its brightness value is above the defined threshold.

remove

Processing and displaying data

This website serves the purpose of processing the captured pictures to calculate the zenith angle and displaying the angle-count chart and the pictures of individual muons with their information. The recorded data is uploaded manually to the website and the angle-count chart is updated with each upload.
